Police Murders in 2015 by Gender

This chart shows the count of police murders by gender through the months of 2015. I used this chart because I believe that it effectively communicates the disparity in gender in police murders, specifically that men are killed at a much higher count by police. I was also curious if there were any visible trends in murder count, and don't see any that behave the same across gender. We are looking at total murder count by gender aggregated by month. To better understand this dynamic, it would help to have data on encounter count by gender, to examine if men are murdered at a higher rate per encounter with police, or if it is a similar rate but men are confronted by police at a higher rate.

Race in Police Murders

This donut chart shows the amount of people murdered by police each in 2015, grouped by race. This visual helps to demonstrate how race plays a factor in police violence. I am examining count aggregated by race. I chose to use a pie chart because it effectively demonstrates how large groups are to one another, and what part of the whole they makeup. Were I to have additional data, I would want racial data on police stops, to understand what the distribution is of stops by race, as well as the crime committed for the person killed, to analyze if that had any interplay with race.

Mental Illness in Police Murders

This bar graph shows the distribution of amount of people murdered by police each month that had a mental illness, stacked with people that did not have a mental illness. This visual helps to demonstrate the difference in amount of people that were mentally ill vs not mentally ill, as well as the count of mentally ill individuals killed by the police. These are people that need protecting, not violent reactions. Additional data that would provide context would be what mental illness the individuals had, as well as if they had been treated or were actively on medication at the time.
