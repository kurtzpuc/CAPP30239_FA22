Police Murders in 2015 by Gender

This chart shows the count of police murders by gender through the months of 2015. I used this chart because I believe that it effectively communicates the dispartity in gender in police murders, specifically that men are at much higher risk of murder by police. I was also curious if there were any visible trends in murder count, and don't see any that behave the same across gender. We are looking at total murder count by gender aggregated by month. To better understand this dynamic, it would help to have data on encounter count by gender, to examine if men are murdered at a higher rate per encounter with police, or if it is a similar rate but men are confronted by police at a higher rate.

Mental Illness in Police Murders

This bar graph shows the distribution of amount of people murdered by police each month that had a mental illness, stacked with people that did not have a mental illness. This visual helps to demonstrate the sheer difference in amount of people that were mentally ill vs not mentally ill. This visualization matters, because understanding how mental illness plays into police confrontation is an imperative social issue.
